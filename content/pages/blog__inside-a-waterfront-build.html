Inside a North Coast Build: Almaza Bay

Building on the Mediterranean rewards patience and punishes shortcuts. Here is how one Almaza Bay development came together, from first survey to handover.

Written by Darak Group

A North Coast site gives you everything — light, breeze, an endless horizon — and asks for everything in return. The sand shifts, the season is short, and the sea is a constant, corrosive presence every building must be made to live alongside.

Starting from the water

We designed this development outward from the lagoon. The principal rooms open fully to the beach and pool deck, sliding walls of glass retracting so the living space and terrace become one. Services and structure were kept to the rear, quiet and unseen.

On the coast, the view is the architecture. Everything else gets out of its way.

Detailed for the long term

Every exposed fixing is corrosion-grade; the marina walk, decks and beach club are built to the same standard as the chalets. The development was not finished when the photographs were taken — it was finished when we were confident it would still feel this way after a decade of salt and sun.
